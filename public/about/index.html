About me

I am Riley.

I will be graduating from the MS in Computational Analysis and Public Policy (MSCAPP) program at the University of Chicago in June of 2026. MSCAPP is a joint program between the Department of Computer Science and the Harris School of Public Policy. Previously, I studied math and statistics, doing exploratory data analysis on stability properties of certain ecological dynamic models.

I would be delighted to work for a(n)…

  • Local, county, state government
  • Port authority
  • Public service commission
  • Consumer advocacy board
  • Advocacy nonprofit

in the northwest USA, or even around Chicago.

A handful of topics here in the US that get me Riled up…

  • Wealth inequality (trying to look into stuff like alleged millionaire “tax flight” and the scary prospect of a sales tax in Montana)
  • Maritime shipping (I interned with the Great Lakes Governors and Premiers)
  • Rate-regulated electric utilities (e.g. why investor-owned utilities misbehave)
  • Election and government reform (e.g voting systems, replacing the Electoral College, the political-industrial revolving door)
  • Whatever my first job entails

If you please, take a gander at my resume.

A handful of (other) things I find pretty neat…

  • Mathematical biology: Computational neuroscience especially; learning about the Hodgin-Huxley model for the first time felt like such a neat overlap of physics, math, and bio
  • Some video games: I’m always drawn to cool emergent gameplay, particularly in games with co-op, skill trees / specialization, and physics. Barotrauma is probably the archetypal game for what I mean.
  • Melon Fanta: Not watermelon, but melon (honeydew). Not ramune, but Fanta.
  • Spanish language: There’s something about learning vocabulary that’s so inherently fun, and some way or another I find grammar pretty neat too. It’s just a matter of actually speaking and listening now…
